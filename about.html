Who we are

Built for the people who move industries.

Industry Alert is a B2B intelligence newswire serving executives, operators, and investors across the five industries that define the modern economy — AI, Fintech, Health, Energy, and Marketing. We exist to turn the relentless volume of industry news into focused, decision-ready intelligence.

Our mission

The modern executive is drowning in information. Thousands of press releases, earnings calls, regulatory filings, and analyst reports compete for attention every single day. Industry Alert was founded on a simple belief: the people who make consequential decisions deserve a better signal.

We built a newsroom focused entirely on B2B verticals — not general business news, not consumer trends. Every story we publish is selected because it matters to someone running a business, managing a portfolio, or leading a team in one of these five sectors. Our editorial filter is rigorous: if it doesn't change how a decision-maker thinks or acts, it doesn't make the briefing.

Our editorial voice is trusted because it is independent. We take no money from the companies we cover in exchange for favorable coverage. Our journalists are vertical specialists, not generalists — they bring deep domain fluency to every story. And we publish every weekday morning, so your intelligence is fresh when your workday begins.

Editorial standards

Our readers trust us because we hold ourselves to standards that put their interests first. Here is how we work.

  • 1
    Editorial independence

    Our coverage is never influenced by advertisers, sponsors, or commercial relationships. Revenue from advertising and press release distribution funds our journalism but does not direct it. No payment to Industry Alert ever secures favorable coverage, and our newsroom operates with a strict separation between editorial and business functions.

  • 2
    Sourcing standards

    We rely on primary sources — company filings, regulatory documents, original interviews, and verified data — whenever possible. We clearly label speculation, analysis, and opinion to distinguish them from reported fact. Anonymous sources are used sparingly and only when the information is newsworthy and cannot be obtained on the record.

  • 3
    Corrections policy

    When we make an error, we correct it promptly and transparently. Corrections appear at the top of the affected article with a clear description of what changed and when. We do not quietly edit published stories without notation. If you believe we have made a factual error, contact our editorial team directly and we will investigate immediately.
