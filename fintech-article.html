Federal Reserve Launches FedNow 2.0 With Real-Time Settlement for Corporate Payments Over $1M

The upgraded instant payment rail removes the previous $500K transaction ceiling, opening the system to corporate treasury, M&A escrow, and interbank settlement workflows — and threatening SWIFT's dominance in high-value domestic corridors.

Sofia Reyes MAY 27, 2026 · 7 min read

Key Takeaways

  • FedNow 2.0 raises the per-transaction ceiling from $500K to $10M, effective immediately for all 900+ connected financial institutions.
  • Real-time gross settlement replaces the previous deferred net settlement model for high-value transactions, eliminating overnight counterparty risk.
  • SWIFT domestic wire volume fell 8% in the week following the announcement, with JPMorgan and Bank of America leading the migration.
  • The Fed confirms FedNow 2.0 will support ISO 20022 structured data natively — a critical requirement for corporate ERP integrations.

The Federal Reserve quietly upgraded its FedNow instant payment system on Monday, raising the per-transaction limit from $500,000 to $10 million and enabling real-time gross settlement for high-value corporate flows. The move, which applies immediately to all 900-plus financial institutions connected to the network, fundamentally changes the calculus for corporate treasury teams that have continued to rely on SWIFT and Fedwire for large-value domestic transactions despite faster alternatives being available.

The previous $500K ceiling was the primary reason corporate treasury and M&A teams bypassed FedNow in favor of Fedwire — which settles in real time but operates on a different cost and operational model — or SWIFT, which handles high-value domestic corridors through correspondent banking arrangements. With $10M per transaction now available, an estimated 94% of corporate wire transfers that currently flow through SWIFT's domestic channels fall within FedNow's new limits, according to Fed analysis.

"The transaction limit was always an artificial constraint on what the network could technically handle. This upgrade puts FedNow in direct competition with Fedwire for the first time — and the pricing difference is substantial."

ISO 20022: The Structural Upgrade That Matters More

Buried in the technical release notes is a change that payments professionals say will prove more consequential than the limit increase: native ISO 20022 structured data support. Previous FedNow transactions carried limited remittance information — enough for consumer P2P but insufficient for corporate workflows that require invoice matching, purchase order references, and VAT breakdowns to flow alongside payment instructions. ISO 20022 support enables rich structured data in the same message, eliminating the manual reconciliation step that has been the primary operational friction point for enterprise adoption.

Three of the five largest US banks confirmed they are actively migrating high-value domestic wire workflows to FedNow 2.0, with full cutover timelines ranging from 90 to 180 days. The correspondent banking community is watching closely: smaller regional banks that earn fee income routing high-value wires through larger correspondents face significant revenue pressure if direct FedNow connectivity becomes the default for corporate clients. The Fed has indicated it will not adjust its tiered pricing model in response — FedNow transaction fees remain capped at $0.045 per transaction regardless of value, versus Fedwire's $0.83 per transaction for amounts above $250K.
